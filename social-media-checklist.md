# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional.** Use your real name and a clear profile photo on academic/professional platforms. Future employers and professors may look you up.

2. **Review privacy settings regularly.** On platforms like Instagram and Facebook, set posts to "Friends Only" or review what is publicly visible at least once per semester.

3. **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or a recruiter saw this?" If the answer is no, do not post it.

4. **Give credit when sharing others' content.** Always tag the original creator or mention the source when reposting articles, photos, or work that is not your own.

5. **Use social media for learning.** Follow educators, researchers, and organisations in your field. Platforms like LinkedIn and YouTube can be powerful self-learning tools.

6. **Report harassment or inappropriate content.** Every platform has a reporting mechanism. Use it without hesitation if you encounter bullying, threats, or harmful content.

7. **Maintain a consistent, positive digital identity.** What you post across platforms creates a cumulative impression. Be intentional about what that impression communicates.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Do not share personal information publicly.** Your phone number, home address, daily schedule, or college ID should never appear in public posts.

2. **Do not post during moments of strong negative emotion.** Angry, impulsive posts are very difficult to take back and can damage relationships and reputation permanently.

3. **Do not plagiarise or misrepresent others' work.** Sharing someone else's project, writing, or photo without credit is both unethical and, in many cases, illegal.

4. **Do not click suspicious links sent via DMs.** Social media is a common channel for phishing attacks. Never enter your password through a link received via message, even from a known contact whose account may have been compromised.

5. **Do not overshare your location in real time.** Posting "Live from [Place]" while you are still there can create safety risks. Share location-based content after you have left, if at all.

6. **Do not engage in or ignore cyberbullying.** Neither participating in trolling nor silently watching it is acceptable. Speak up or report it.

7. **Do not use your college email for non-academic social media signups.** Reserve your college email for educational platforms, GitHub, and official communications.
